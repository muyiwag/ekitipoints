Ekiti Points of Interest

About

In January 2011, the newly elected Governor of Ekiti State, Dr. Kayode Fayemi, requested an NGO (Development Support Initiatives or DSI) to organize a Free Health Mission in fulfillment of his campaign promises to the people of the state.

DSI traveled to all 16 local government areas (LGAs) of Ekiti State with about 150 medical personnel and stayed in each town for a full day. They were ultimately able to treat over 100,000 citizens with a variety of conditions

During the one-day Free Health Mission in each LGA, the DSI Data Team would fan out into the surrounding neighborhoods with GPS recorders and clipboards in order to collect health-related points of interest (POIs) such as water sources (boreholes, wells, streams), primary health centers, pharmacies, public toilets and refuse sites.

These POIs are important for epidemiological investigations; for example, locations of water sources can be used to trace the roots of many common water-borne diseases such as guineaworm or water blindness.

The map above shows the locations of these POIs in selected towns.

How It Works

  1. Drag the map with the cursor to move to a town of interest
  2. Use the zoom controls (buttons labeled '+' and '-') to zoom in on the town of interest
  3. The POIs are labeled with icons (e.g. a cross represents a hospital, a water drop represents a water source).
  4. Hold your cursor over the icon for the POI and a box will appear showing details of that POI.
  5. Add a stamp and mail in your form
